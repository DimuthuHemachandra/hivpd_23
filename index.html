HIVPD_23 Research Project

Advancing Understanding of Neurological Outcomes in HIV and Parkinson's Disease

A comprehensive multi-center study investigating the neurological pathways and imaging biomarkers associated with HIV-related cognitive impairment and Parkinson's disease. Our research combines cutting-edge MRI technology with rigorous clinical protocols to advance treatment strategies and improve patient outcomes.

Project Goals

Our primary research objectives aimed at advancing neurological research

Identify Biomarkers

Identify neuroimaging biomarkers associated with cognitive decline in HIV-positive individuals and Parkinson's disease patients using advanced MRI techniques.

Understand Mechanisms

Investigate the neurological pathways and mechanisms underlying the intersection of HIV infection and neurodegenerative processes.

Build Data Repository

Establish a comprehensive, high-quality dataset of neuroimaging and clinical data for future research and validation studies.

Develop Predictive Models

Create predictive models for early detection of cognitive decline and disease progression using machine learning approaches.

Inform Treatment

Provide evidence-based insights to guide therapeutic interventions and improve treatment outcomes for affected populations.

Foster Collaboration

Promote interdisciplinary collaboration among neurologists, radiologists, infectious disease specialists, and data scientists.

Inclusion & Exclusion Criteria

Participant eligibility requirements for the study

Inclusion Criteria

  • Age: Adults aged 18-75 years
  • HIV Status: Confirmed HIV-positive diagnosis with documented viral load
  • Treatment: On stable antiretroviral therapy (ART) for ≥6 months
  • Cognitive Function: Able to provide informed consent
    Participants must demonstrate capacity to understand study procedures, risks, and benefits, as assessed by research staff using standardized consent evaluation protocols.
  • Language: Fluent in English or Spanish
  • Availability: Willing to complete all study visits and MRI scans
  • Documentation: Medical records available for review
    Participants must provide access to relevant medical history, including HIV diagnosis date, CD4 counts, viral load history, ART regimen details, and neurological assessments.

Exclusion Criteria

  • MRI Safety: Contraindications to MRI scanning
  • Neurological: History of major neurological disorders
    Excludes stroke, traumatic brain injury with loss of consciousness >30 minutes, brain tumors, multiple sclerosis, or other conditions that could confound neuroimaging results.
  • Psychiatric: Active psychotic disorder or severe mental illness
  • Substance Use: Current substance dependence
    Active alcohol or drug dependence within the past 6 months, as defined by DSM-5 criteria. Participants in stable recovery programs may be considered on a case-by-case basis.
  • Pregnancy: Pregnant or nursing women
  • Opportunistic Infections: Active CNS infections
  • Other Research: Concurrent enrollment in conflicting studies

Data Management Protocols

Comprehensive procedures for data collection, storage, and security

Our data management strategy ensures the highest standards of data quality, security, and compliance with regulatory requirements including HIPAA, GDPR, and NIH data sharing policies.

Data Collection

  • REDCap electronic data capture system
  • Standardized case report forms (CRFs)
  • Real-time data validation rules
  • Automated timestamp logging
  • Source document verification

Security Measures

  • AES-256 encryption at rest and in transit
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
  • Role-based access control (RBAC)
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • SOC 2 Type II certified infrastructure

Privacy Protection

  • De-identification of all participant data
  • Unique study IDs (no PHI in research datasets)
  • Limited dataset sharing agreements
  • IRB-approved data use agreements
  • Regular privacy impact assessments

Data Access

  • Tiered access levels based on role
  • Audit logs for all data access
  • Data use agreements required
  • Annual recertification for access
  • Collaborative access via secure portal

Storage & Backup

  • AWS cloud infrastructure with redundancy
  • Daily automated backups
  • Disaster recovery plan tested quarterly
  • Minimum 7-year data retention
  • Geographic replication for resilience

Quality Assurance

  • Independent data monitoring committee
  • Regular data quality reports
  • Site monitoring visits
  • Query resolution workflows
  • Statistical validation procedures

MRI Data Protocols

Standardized imaging acquisition and processing procedures

Our neuroimaging protocol employs state-of-the-art MRI sequences to capture structural, functional, and microstructural brain changes with the highest quality and reproducibility.

  • Field Strength: 3.0 Tesla
  • Manufacturers: Siemens Prisma, GE Discovery MR750
  • Head Coil: 32-channel phased array
  • Gradient Strength: 80 mT/m maximum
  • Slew Rate: 200 T/m/s

All sites use harmonized scanning protocols to ensure data compatibility

  • Sequence: 3D MPRAGE (Magnetization Prepared Rapid Gradient Echo)
  • TR/TE/TI: 2300/2.98/900 ms
  • Flip Angle: 9°
  • Voxel Size: 1.0 × 1.0 × 1.0 mm³ (isotropic)
  • Matrix: 256 × 256 × 176
  • Acquisition Time: ~6 minutes
  • Purpose: Volumetric analysis, cortical thickness, subcortical segmentation
  • Sequence: Multi-shell diffusion imaging
  • b-values: 0, 1000, 2000 s/mm²
  • Directions: 30 directions per shell (90 total + 10 b=0)
  • Voxel Size: 2.0 × 2.0 × 2.0 mm³
  • Slices: 70 axial slices (whole brain)
  • TR/TE: 3500/95 ms
  • Acquisition Time: ~12 minutes
  • Analysis: DTI metrics (FA, MD, AD, RD), tractography, connectivity
  • Sequence: Multi-band EPI (echo-planar imaging)
  • TR/TE: 800/30 ms
  • Voxel Size: 2.0 × 2.0 × 2.0 mm³
  • Volumes: 480 (resting state), task-dependent for fMRI
  • Multi-band Factor: 8
  • Flip Angle: 52°
  • Acquisition Time: ~6.5 minutes (resting state)
  • Tasks: Resting state, working memory (N-back), executive function

Automated QC Metrics

  • Signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) assessment
  • Motion artifact detection (framewise displacement)
  • Image contrast and homogeneity checks
  • Ghosting and artifact detection
  • Field map quality assessment

Manual Review

  • Visual inspection by trained neuroradiologist
  • Incidental findings protocol
  • Inter-rater reliability checks (10% sample)

Scans failing QC are repeated within 2 weeks when possible

Preprocessing Steps

  • DICOM to NIfTI: Conversion using dcm2niix
  • Brain Extraction: FSL BET or HD-BET
  • Motion Correction: FSL MCFLIRT
  • Distortion Correction: FSL TOPUP (field maps)
  • Spatial Normalization: ANTs registration to MNI152 space
  • Intensity Normalization: N4 bias field correction

Analysis Software

  • FreeSurfer: Cortical parcellation and volumetrics
  • FSL: Diffusion and functional MRI analysis
  • ANTs: Registration and normalization
  • AFNI: Functional connectivity analysis
  • Custom Python: Quality control and statistical analysis

Data Storage Format

  • Standard: Brain Imaging Data Structure (BIDS)
  • Format: NIfTI-1 (.nii.gz)
  • Metadata: JSON sidecars with acquisition parameters

Standardized documentation and data structure

BIDS Compliance

All neuroimaging data follows the Brain Imaging Data Structure (BIDS) specification v1.8.0, ensuring interoperability and facilitating automated analysis pipelines.

Variable Naming

Standardized variable naming conventions ensure consistency across all data collection instruments and databases.

  • Format: snake_case for all variables
  • Dates: YYYY-MM-DD (ISO 8601)
  • IDs: sub-XXX format (zero-padded)
  • Missing: -999 (numeric), "NA" (text)
  • Boolean: 0/1 or TRUE/FALSE
